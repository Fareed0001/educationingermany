Scholarships

Germany stands out as one of the most budget-friendly destinations in Europe for international students pursuing education. Renowned for its reputable and cost-effective education system, top-tier universities, and outstanding infrastructure, Germany attracts approximately 350,000 international students annually. The overall cost of studying in Germany is reasonable, yet many international students face challenges covering living expenses, which can amount to around EUR 1200 per month. Fortunately, there are numerous financial aid opportunities available for international students, with scholarships being a significant resource. Here are some of the popular Germany Government scholarship programs:

1. DAAD Scholarship Germany
DAAD Scholarship Germany is offered by The German Academic Exchange Service (DAAD) for international students to study in Germany. DAAD Scholarships are very popular and the organization offers 215 scholarships and funding opportunities to international students. These scholarships aid more than 100,000 German and international students as well as researchers. DAAD scholarships are among the fully funded scholarship in Germany.

2. Erasmus Scholarship Programs in Germany
Erasmus Scholarship Germany is the largest scholarship provider in the EU and it is funded by the European Commission. Under Erasmus scholarship, the average monthly grant offered to a student can go up to 350 EUR. Erasmus scholarship Germany provides funding to international students to study in the countries that come under the European Union.

Non-government funded scholarships simply mean that such scholarships are not funded by the government, but other entities. Here is a list of some the popular non-governmental scholarships for Germany :

1. Heinrich Boll Scholarships for International Students
The Heinrich Boll Foundation provides scholarships to international students, including refugees, at all the degree levels. The foundation mainly awards scholarships to students who plan to study STEM fields (Science, technology, engineering, mathematics) and especially programs focusing on Environmental sciences. Other scholarships are awarded to students planning to study journalism, economic or legal studies and art degrees.

2. Bayer Foundations
Bayer Foundation awards scholarships to students who are planning to undertake various research projects. The scholarship is given to both German and international students. The only requirement for students is that they must provide excellent academic results and the research project must focus on an innovative topic and approach.

3. Konrad-Adenauer-Stiftung (KAS) Scholarships
International students who plan to study masters or PhD in Germany are eligible for the scholarship. The applicant must be under 30 years of age and must have completed a university degree in their home country. The applicants must also provide extraordinary academic scores and should be actively involved in voluntary work that benefits the society in their home country.

4. The Friedrich Ebert Stiftung
This organization awards scholarships to German and international students having an inclination towards social and political matters. The applicants must have above average academic results and must show a sense of civil responsibility. Students can receive upto 1000 EUR per month which can cover their enrolment fees, health insurance and monthly required books and other study materials.

5. Mawista Scholarship
These scholarships are offered to applicants who are planning to study abroad while taking care of a child. The amount granted is around EUR 500 per month.

6. Marie Curie International Incoming Fellowships (IIF) For Developing Countries
These scholarships are open to researchers moving within Europe from other parts of the world. These are provided to international students planning to pursue postdoctoral research in Germany. Amount granted can reach up to EUR 15,000 per year.

Students can find various Germany University scholarships which can provide them a little financial support. Take a look at some of the scholarships offered by universities in Germany:

1. Heidelberg University Scholarships
Heidelberg University is one of the most popular universities in Germany. It offers various scholarships to international students from various fields. Examples include international ambassador scholarship for undergraduate students. This scholarship is awarded upon admission and is based on the studentâ€™s academic performance in high school.

2. Hamburg University of Applied Sciences Scholarships
Hamburg University offers scholarships to international students of upto EUR 400 per month, four months or semester.

3. RWTH Aachen University Scholarships
The RWTH University offers many scholarships to students which can provide various financial benefits. The university will cover upto 50% of tuition fees of the selected students who get the scholarship.

4. Humboldt University International Research Fellowship
Humboldt University offers fellowship for international students pursuing research in history, anthropology, law, sociology, political science, geography, economics or area studies.

5. Leipzig Graduate School of Management Scholarship
Leipzig Graduate School of Management offers scholarships or financial aids for students enrolled in various MSc courses

6. Frankfurt School of Finance and Management Doctoral Tuition Waivers
This scholarship at Frankfurt School of Finance is offered to doctoral students studying an English-taught program at the University. It is applicable for students studying mathematics, finance, accounting, and business administration.

Scholarships for Germany are available for every type of degree be it bachelors, masters or doctorate degrees. Take a look at some of the common scholarships for every type of degree:

1. Scholarship For Bachelor Degree in Germany
Bachelors degrees in Germany are offered for free i.e students do not have to pay tuition fees, except in the state of Baden-Wurttemberg. However, students do have to pay semester fees after completion of every semester. The semester fee may vary depending on the university.

  • Ambedkar Overseas Vidya Nidhi Scholarship for SC 2021 (For Indian nationals)
  • Domestic and International state grants at Munich Business School
  • DAAD Bavarian Government Scholarship at Hochschule Hof, Germany
  • KAS Foundation Scholarship, Germany
  • Leaky Foundation: Joan Cogswell Donner Scholarship for Students worldwide
You can find various other scholarship for bachelor degree in Germany. These scholarships offer partial funding or full funding. You may apply for any scholarship in Germany for undergraduates that you find helpful. The scholarships will definitely help you bring down your living costs as an undergraduate student in Germany.

Hence there are various scholarships for master in Germany. The scholarships may be fully funded or partially funded.

PhD Scholarships in Germany
